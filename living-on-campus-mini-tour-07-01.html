ORIGINAL: The Leo Goodwin Sr. Residence Hall is our primary freshmen residence hall and houses up to 292 undergraduate students. Each room is built for double or single occupancy and features a bathroom, large closet space, and high ceilings. The Goodwin Residence Hall has limited availability for single rooms, so if you're interested in living here and having your own room, you will be considered on a first-come-first served basis. Each room is furnished with beds, desks, desk chairs, dressers, and a built-in storage/counter facility. The hall includes classroom space, a rec room, study lounges, a laundry room, a common kitchen area, and a large TV lounge.

The Rolling Hills Graduate Apartments complex houses nearly 400 graduate and doctoral students. Each single and quad room is fully furnished and features a kitchen, bathroom, and living room. One of the main features is the Rolling Hills Residential Leisure Pool. The pool is beautifully landscaped with a waterfall and a "zero entry" point to the north. It's a great facility for relaxing, hanging out, or studying with an indoor lounge area and an outdoor covered pavilion! This resort-style pool is an exclusive benefit for grad students living in the Rolling Hills apartments.
